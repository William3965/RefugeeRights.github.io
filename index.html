The occurrence of Refugees has been going on for a very long time. No matter in what era, there is always a conflict. The war and violence caused many people to flee their country, and the matter has been around for a very long time. One of the first record of refugees was from 1687 (Huegunots). Many had to flee from their home to escape persecutions, and since then it has been growing in numbers. Until World War II strikes, where we saw the most number of refugees ever. This issue had to be resolved by the United Nations, where they felt the need to create international guidelines relating to protection and acceptance of refugees. Then an amendment was made in 1967 to increase the scope of the document to include refugees fleeing any life-threatening situation worldwide, rather than relating exclusively to pre-1951 conflicts in Europe. However, with all the guidelines and help from many organizations, Refugees are still faced with a lot of problems.

In 2020, a report from The International Organization for Migration (IOM), a part of the United Nations promoting humane and orderly migration for the benefit of all, claimed that over 281 million people around the world are migrants, which equates to 3.6% of the world population. TRAC Immigration also mentioned that there is about 1.5 million asylum seekers based in the US alone. And while there’s an indefinite amount on how many people died while immigrating via Water transport, a statistic from Statista shows that 2,062 people died in 2022 while doing so. Nonetheless, there are always help to be given, The United Nations Relief and Works Agency for Palestine Refugees (UNRWA), United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR), The British Red Cross are trying to tackle the problem of refugees, namely providing asylums, healthcare, setting charities to support migrants and etc.
